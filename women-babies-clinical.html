Perineal Care and Repair

Description
This is a half-day course where participants will be given lots of opportunity to learn the theory behind perineal repair care and practice suturing skills required through simulation. This course is pitched at a beginner level and is a prerequiste for perineal repair accreditation.

Objectives
By the end of this course participants should:
  • Identify anatomy and physiology of the perineum and classifications of perineal trauma
  • Identify strategies to help protect perineal integrity and limiting perineal trauma in childbirth
  • Identify the role of perineal suturing in continuity of Midwifery care
  • Identify the different uses and properties of common suture material
  • Identify the selection and use of local anaesthetics and infiltration techniques
  • Identify clinical need for and correct technique when performing an episiostomy
  • Demonstrate basic perineal repair techniques, use of instruments and knot tying
  • Demonstrate the application of infection control and WHS principles in repair of the perineum
  • Identify documentation requirements
  • Identify postpartum care after perineal trauma and repair
Audience
Midwives and Obstetric staff

Speculum Examination Workshop

Description
To provide midwives with the skill to perform a speculum examination

Objectives
By the end of this course participants should:
  • Participants will understand the indications for performing a sterile speculum examination of the term pregnant woman
  • Participants will participate in simulated speculum examination under the supervision of the facilitator
  • Participants will be able to describe instances where it is appropriate to use a speculum in a pregnant woman
Audience
Midwives

Women and Babies 05

Course aim

All employees working within a health care environment will at some point come in contact with people who are distressed; this will be more prevalent in some professions than others.

Supporting and communicating with people who are distressed can involve the use of basic counselling skills. Examples of this can include: helping patients/colleagues experiencing emotional, social and relationship problems, helping families face a crisis, taking a patient history, helping patients to cope with their treatment, providing reassurance to others, dealing with anger and fear, helping patients deal with their illness and simply supporting each other.

The basis of counselling is good communication skills. How effective is your communication? Is it time for a refresher?

Course objectives

On completion of this workshop participants will be able to:

Identify:

  • Parameters for referral to a specialist counsellor.
  • Personal attributes of a good counsellor.
  • Roles and responsibilities of the counsellor.
  • The use of general counselling/interviewing skills in your clinical role.
  • The micro skills used in counselling/interviewing: attending , questioning, client observation skills, active listening, responding, reflecting feelings, problem solving, closure.

Demonstrate:

  • The individual basic micro skills in counselling.
  • Integrating micro skills into a basic counselling session.

Analyse:

  • Boundaries in counselling.
  • Personal factors that may impact on the effectiveness of counselling.
Target group
  • Health Care Workers & Health Professionals
  • All clinical staff
  • Managers and administrative officers who have an interest in improving their communication skills
