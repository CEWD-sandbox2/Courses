Advanced Emergency Practice (AEP)

Description

To provide Emergency Department nurses an opportunity to gain Advanced Emergency clinical skills and knowledge to initiate care for patients waiting to be seen by a Medical Officer and then to implement definitive management of patients using the Clinical Initiatives Nurse (CIN) protocols. The course involves face to face lectures, case studies, clinical simulation and interactive workshops.

Course objectives

By the end of this course participants should:

  • Demonstrate an advanced level of clinical competency in the care of patients outlined in the NSW Health CIN role using theoretical knowledge and clinical judgement by successfully completing the program assessments.
  • Effectively assess patients on presentation to ED as outlined in the NSW Health CIN role and independently initiate investigations based on this assessment.
  • Utilise advanced skills in emergency nursing including advanced assessment skills; initiation of x-rays, investigations & analgesia based on these assessment skills; and implement patient care using advanced clinical skills such as wound closure techniques, fracture management and invasive procedures (e.g. arterial blood sampling).
  • Identify and comprehend the potential complications related to peripheral venous cannulation / venepuncture.
Audience

Emergency Department Nurses

Adult Advanced Life Support

Description

This is a level 1 ALS course designed for new/junior critical care staff who have not been accredited before. It is for staff wanting to enhance their knowledge and skills in advanced life support techniques for those responding to cardiac arrests within a CERS/ICU assist team. This course provides the foundations to go on to become an accredited ALS provider within SLHD.

Course Objectives

  • Outline the A-G assessment and recognition of the deteriorating patient
  • Demonstrate competent airway management with BMV
  • Demonstrate safe defibrillation using the coached acronym
  • Identify shockable and non-shockable rhythms and their appropriate management using the COACHED acronym
  • Outline first line pharmacological treatment within a cardiac arrest
  • Discuss reversible causes in ALS
  • Discuss rational for transcutaneous pacing and demonstrate skill in simulated environment
  • Discuss human factors and graded assertiveness in critical situations
  • Demonstrate team roles in a simulated ALS scenario

Audience

Staff working in SLHD critical care areas only or Resuscitation team members

Staff wishing to attend

  • Must have Basic Life Support accreditation
  • Must be working in a critical care area
  • Must have working knowledge of the defibrillator/arrest trolley their workplace

Emergency Management of the Seriously Ill Child (EMSIC)

Description

This program has been developed to assist nursing and medical staff working in Sydney Local Health District to recognise seriously ill infants and children. It aims to provide the participants with knowledge on how to recognise a seriously ill or injured child and manage the treatment of the child using a structured approach based on current best practice guidelines. The course is a compulsory component of the 'Transition to Emergency Nursing' program.

Objectives

This course will enable participants to:

  • Describe basic life support for the infant, child and older child and prioritise care within best practice guidelines
  • Outline / Identify signs and symptoms of the deteriorating infant /child
  • Differentiate anatomy and physiology of adults and children and outline appropriate care for specific populations
  • Develop strategies for the initial management of the seriously ill or critically injured child
  • Perform a rapid core assessment of airway, breathing, circulatory and neurological function in the seriously ill or injured child.
Audience

Registered Nurses, Enrolled Nurses and Junior Medical Officers who work in Emergency Departments.

Transition into Emergency Nursing Program - Basic Emergency Assessment (Part I)

Description

This course is a component of the Transition to Emergency Nursing program. The day aims to provide new nurses working in emergency department and fundamental knowledge and skills to complete a rapid core assessment within the context of the emergency patient.

Objectives

By the end of this course participants should:

  • Identify the emergency nursing models of care in the context for caring for a patient in the Emergency setting
  • Demonstrate how to perform a rapid core assessment utilising the primary and secondary survey frameworks to monitor patient health status
  • Describe how to assess an airway and identify appropriate management of a ventilated patient with basic airway manoeurves, airway adjuncts and breathing apparatus
  • Identify life threatening arrhythmias and utilise a systematic approach to cardiac rhythm strip interpretation
  • Demonstrate sytematic neurological assessment and prioritise care of emergency patients at threat of neurological compromise
  • Explain the significance of fluid and electrolyte balance and identify fluid resuscitation principles.
Audience

Emergency Department Nurses

Transition to Emergency Nursing Program - Common Presenting Problems (Part II)

Description

The course aims to provide new nurses working in Emergency Departments with the fundemental knowledge and skills to assess and iniate care for common emergency presentaions. Delivery utilises presentations from expert clinicians, workshops, case studies and simulation.

Objectives

By the end of this course participants should:

  • Analyse the clinical indicators of urgency associated with common presentations and select the most appropriate nursing assessment and intervention for positive outcomes
  • Describe the clinical pathways for common emergency presentations and identify policy directives for specific patient groups
  • Identify situations where and when cardiac assessment would be indicated and use strategies for interpreting 12 lead ECG's, chest x-rays and blood gases.
  • Describe the indication for limb immobilisation and demonstrate ability to measure and fit common splinting devices.
Audience

Triage Nursing

Description
The aim of this course is to provide Registered Nurses with the knowledge and skills to safely and competently perform the role of the triage nurse. Delivery of this course includes presentations from expert nurses, interactive scenarios and clinical simulation.

Objectives
By the end of this course participants should:
  • Analyse appropriate referral services to those patients who present to ED and select the most appropriate disposition or treatment area in ED context.
  • Describe the components of a rapid assessment of a patient using best practice principles within a 3-5 minute time frame and use strategies to apply the Australian Triage Scale to all patients that present to ED.
  • Identify priorities of care for patients that present to ED to discuss and perform emergency interventions and first aid.
Audience
Registered Nurses working in SLHD Emergency Departments

Venepuncture & Cannulation Pre-accreditation

Description

The aim of this course is to acquire knowledge and skills in peripheral venous cannulation and venepuncture in order to maintain quality patient care through prompt administration of prescribed intravenous medications or fluids and prevent any delay in patient blood investigations.

Participants are required to complete pre-course work book at least 2 weeks prior to the workshop. You are required to access the Sydney Local Health District Education Support Portal (ESP).  You need to login to the Peripheral Venous Cannulation and Venepuncture Resources and complete the Peripheral Venous Cannulation and Venepuncture Pre-accreditation (must achieve 100%). 

Objectives

This course will enable participants to:

  • apply the SLHD policy & procedures relating to peripheral venous cannulation and/or venepuncture
  • adhere to infection control policy and standard precautions when accessing peripheral veins for cannulation and/or venepuncture
  • demonstrate competency in the performance of peripheral venous cannulation using safety cannula and venepuncture using the vacutainer system
  • identify and comprehend the potential complications related to peripheral venous cannulation / venepuncture
Audience

Registered Nurses, Registered Midwives, Enrolled Nurses and Registered Professionals with AHPRA who are required to perform IV cannulation and/or venepuncture as part of their role.
