Adult Venepuncture Cannulation Pre-accreditation

Course aim

The aim of this course is to acquire knowledge and skills in peripheral venous cannulation and venepuncture in order to maintain quality patient care through prompt administration of prescribed intravenous medications or fluids and prevent any delay in patient blood investigations.

Course requirements

Participants are required to complete pre-course work book at least 2 weeks prior to the workshop. You are required to access the Sydney Local Health District Education Support Portal (ESP). You need to login to the Peripheral Venous Cannulation and Venepuncture Resources and complete the Peripheral Venous Cannulation and Venepuncture Pre-accreditation (must achieve 100%).

Course objectives
  • Apply the SLHD policy & procedures relating to peripheral venous cannulation and/or venepuncture.
  • Adhere to infection control policy and standard precautions when accessing peripheral veins for cannulation and/or venepuncture.
  • Demonstrate competency in the performance of peripheral venous cannulation using safety cannula and venepuncture using the vacutainer system.
  • Identify and comprehend the potential complications related to peripheral venous cannulation / venepuncture.
Target group
  • Registered Nurses
  • Registered Midwives
  • Enrolled Nurses
  • Registered Professionals with AHPRA who are required to perform IV cannulation and/or venepuncture as part of their role

Balmain Hospital Wound Care Workshop (Balmain Hospital Staff only)

Course aim

The aim of this course is o reinforce the fact that wound management is not just about selecting the correct dressing but utilising a collaborative, multidisciplinary team approach, that includes a comprehensive assessment, implementation of evidence based principles of wound management, pressure ulcer prevention and management as well as continual evaluation of the wound healing process..

Course objectives
  • To describe the normal wound healing process in order to identify when a wound is no longer following the normal healing trajectory.
  • To discuss the importance of a holistic assessment of the patient, their wound and their environment.
  • To describe the principles of wound management.
  • To list the clinical features, indications, limitations and guidelines for the more frequently used dressing categories.
  • To provide relevant up-to-date resources for wound management.
  • Discuss pressure injury assessment, prevention and management of pressure injuries in accordance with the SLHD Pressure Injury Policy.
  • Document the assessment, prevention and management of pressure injuries according to the SLHD policies.
  • Discuss the management of skin tears.
Target group
  • Registered Nurses
  • Registered Midwives
  • Enrolled Nurses
  • Endorsed Enrolled Nurses
  • Community Nurses
  • CNCs
  • CNSs
  • CNEs

Counselling for Non Counsellors

Course aim

All employees working within a health care environment will at some point come in contact with people who are distressed; this will be more prevalent in some professions than others.

Supporting and communicating with people who are distressed can involve the use of basic counselling skills. Examples of this can include: helping patients/colleagues experiencing emotional, social and relationship problems, helping families face a crisis, taking a patient history, helping patients to cope with their treatment, providing reassurance to others, dealing with anger and fear, helping patients deal with their illness and simply supporting each other.

The basis of counselling is good communication skills. How effective is your communication? Is it time for a refresher?

Course objectives

On completion of this workshop participants will be able to:

Identify:

  • Parameters for referral to a specialist counsellor.
  • Personal attributes of a good counsellor.
  • Roles and responsibilities of the counsellor.
  • The use of general counselling/interviewing skills in your clinical role.
  • The micro skills used in counselling/interviewing: attending , questioning, client observation skills, active listening, responding, reflecting feelings, problem solving, closure.

Demonstrate:

  • The individual basic micro skills in counselling.
  • Integrating micro skills into a basic counselling session.

Analyse:

  • Boundaries in counselling.
  • Personal factors that may impact on the effectiveness of counselling.
Target group
  • Health Care Workers & Health Professionals
  • All clinical staff
  • Managers and administrative officers who have an interest in improving their communication skills

Mental Health Assessment

Course aim

The aim of this course is to enhance clinicians' skills in undertaking a comprehensive mental health assessment and enable them to undertake:

  • Mental Health Triage: risk and vulnerability, Mental State Examination; Psychiatric assessment; Physical and Mental Health Assessment.
  • Strengths based assessment.
  • Engagement skills and communication management.
  • Clinical impression formulation/Differential and provisional diagnosis.
  • Clinical decision making.
  • Documentation: descriptive and narrative; MH-OAT format.
  • Cross cultural assessment.
  • Consumer Perspective.
  • Assessment tools: BPRS, DASS, K10, EPNPS.
  • Care Planning.
Course objectives

This course will enable participants to:

  • Gain an appreciation of consumers who receive an assessment.
  • Recognise the cultural and clinical requirements of undertaking an assessment on a client from a CALD background.
  • Outline and discuss the components of a MSE.
  • Outline and discuss the components of a Risk Assessment.
  • Demonstrate application of both descriptive and narrative documentation.
  • Report findings of clinical tools used in assessment.
  • Recognise their own preferences in clinical decision making.
Target group
  • Nursing staff
  • Allied Health staff who work within an Adult Mental Health Clinical Environment

Perinatal Safety Education - Fetal and Maternal Team Training Workshop

Course aim

This education pathway has 3 Fetal Safety Education Team Training workshop sessions and 3 Maternal Safety Education Team Training workshop sessions.

Course objectives

By the end of this course participants should:

  • Utilise a systematic approach to ensure that a comprehensive assessment is conducted.
  • Identify risk factors and associated physiological responses that may impact on maternal and/or fetal well-being.
  • Assess the individual clinical situation to determine method of FHR monitoring.
  • Consider how human factors can contribute to errors and discuss strategies to address this.
  • Apply understanding of maternal physiology and the physiology of FHR control to Identify, interpret and evaluate the early & later warning signs of fetal deterioration.
  • Use algorithms to guide appropriate care planning, initial management and appropriate escalation when fetal deterioration is identified or suspected.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of how additional tests of well-being can inform care planning.
  • Discuss local Clinical Escalation Response System (CERS).
  • Discuss processes in place for the facilitation of a second clinician review.
  • Discuss the importance of effective communication including the importance of accurate and timely documentation.
Target group
  • Midwives
  • Obstetric Medical Officers

Perineal Care and Repair

This is a half day course where participants will be given lots of oppertunity to learn the theory behind perineal repair care and practice suturing skills required through simulation. This course is pitched at a begineer level and is a prerequiste for perineal repair accreditation.

SafeSide Suicide Prevention Training

Course aim

Violence Prevention and Management - Personal Safety - Community

Course aim

This face-to-face workshop discusses how to recognise escalating conditions and signs of impending violence, and how to apply de-escalation techniques to minimise violence.

The requirements for this training are set out in PD2005_315 - Zero Tolerance Response to Violence in the NSW Health Workplace.

This workshop is targeted at staff working in security and duress response; staff working in extremely high risk environnments including Emergency Department, Mental Health and Community Health; and staff working in locations where a local risk assessment has determined there is a potential for violence to occur.

Course objectives

After completing this training you will be able to apply de-escalation and breakaway techniques as a form of violence prevention and minimisation.

  • Describe the importance of self, environment and others in relation to violence prevention and management.
  • Apply a risk assessment approach to preventing and responding to workplace violence.
  • Identify the role of verbal and non-verbal communication in the prevention and management of violent behaviour.
  • Use de-escalation skills to effectively manage violent behaviour as it occurs in the workplace.
  • Identify and select appropriate response options when confronted with violent individuals.
  • Use evasive techniques to maintain personal safety when responding to a violent person.
  • Implement local workplace policies and procedures regarding the prevention and management of workplace violence.
Target group

This workshop is targeted at staff working in security and duress response; staff working in extremely high risk environnments including:

  • Emergency Department staff
  • Mental Health
  • Community Health
  • Staff working in locations where a local risk assessment has determined there is a potential for violence to occur
