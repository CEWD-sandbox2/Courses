Going back to basics in order to improve: Connecting to ourselves and connecting to others

Course overview

The research literature consistently demonstrates that it is the quality of the therapeutic relationship rather than the specific therapy modality employed that is the major determinant of a positive psychotherapeutic outcome. Neuroscience strongly supports the notion that the human brain is only capable of new learning within a safe interpersonal environment, yet many of us have not received specific training in how to facilitate such an environment. In addition, the majority of the professional development courses offered in our field continue to consist of learning new treatment modalities for specific disorders or psychological problems.


This 1/2-day workshop focuses on the therapeutic relationship and the psychological development of the therapist as a person in order to improve our capacity to provide a safe interpersonal environment for our clients to grow and heal past traumatic experiences. The workshop explores the concepts of the therapeutic alliance, relational mindfulness and countertransference reactions.

Course presenter

Sophia Parnas is a Clinical Psychologist and Psychotherapy Educator who works in SLHD and in private practice. She has a special interest in interpersonal relationships and uses neuroscience, psychology and evolutionary biology in order to help clinicians gain a deeper understanding of who we are and what we need in order to connect more intimately and powerfully in the therapeutic space.
